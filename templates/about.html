About page

The formation of the group grew out of a meeting Gandalf had with Thorin in Bree which kindled Thorin's interest in recapturing his long lost family inheritance. (Thorin's grandfather had been the king of the Lonely Mountain when Smaug came and took it.) Remembering that he had once known an adventurous Hobbit on his travels in the Shire, Gandalf decided to add Bilbo to their company because he knew that stealth and cunning were preferable to force. Gandalf also believed that someone like Bilbo could keep the sometimes prideful and stubborn Dwarves from rash action. The superstitious Dwarves also considered thirteen to be an unlucky number, and as Gandalf had planned to leave on other business, welcomed a fourteenth to fill into their party.

Thorin Oakenshield

In the year TA 2746, Thorin II was born to Dwarven prince Thráin II in the mountain city of The Lonely Mountain. Early in his youth, he and the other Dwarves of the Lonely Mountain were forced to flee by the dragon Smaug in TA 2770. While in exile, he quickly grew to become a capable warrior. This was demonstrated when he participated in the Battle of Azanulbizar in TA 2799. He marched with one of the Dwarven armies beneath the East-gate of Moria. At some point in the fighting, his shield broke, and using an oaken tree branch found on the floor as a shield, he gained the epithet "Oakenshield," which would remain with him even in death.

Fili and Kili

Fíli and Kíli were nephews of Thorin, who was the elder brother of their mother Dís. They were also nephews of Frerin, grandsons of Thráin II, and great-grandsons of Thrór. Fíli's most distinguishing feature was his long nose, the longest of any of the Dwarves in Thorin's company.

Glóin

Glóin, the brother of Óin, was one of Thorin's most trusted companions. Known for his fiery temper and skill with an axe, he also served as the spokesman for the company when interacting with others. In later years, Glóin became the father of Gimli, a key member of the Fellowship of the Ring.

Balin

Balin, cousin to Glóin and Óin, was known for his wisdom and calm demeanor. He served as the second-in-command to Thorin and was often the voice of reason within the company. Balin later became Lord of Moria, where he tragically met his end.

Dwalin

Dwalin, the younger brother of Balin, was a fierce and loyal warrior. Known for his distinctive tattoos and gruff personality, he carried a pair of war hammers and was among the first to stand by Thorin's side in battle.

Ori

Ori was a scribe and chronicler of the company, often seen writing or sketching during their journey. Despite his gentle nature, Ori fought bravely alongside his companions and later played a role in the events of Moria.

Dori

Dori was one of the strongest members of Thorin's company, often tasked with helping Bilbo when he struggled. Known for his patience and politeness, Dori was a key figure in several of the company's escapades.

Nori

Nori, known for his cunning and resourcefulness, was often the most adventurous of Thorin's company. His unique hairstyle and sharp eyes made him a distinctive figure among the Dwarves.

Bifur

Bifur, who had an axe blade embedded in his skull, spoke primarily in the Dwarvish tongue due to his injury. Despite this, he was a loyal companion and fought valiantly alongside the company.

Bofur

Bofur was a cheerful and optimistic member of the company, always ready with a joke or a kind word. He played a crucial role in keeping the morale of the group high during their journey.

Bombur

Bombur, known for his large size and hearty appetite, often provided comedic relief during the journey. Despite his appearance, he proved himself a capable and courageous member of Thorin's company.

Gandalf

Gandalf the Grey, a powerful wizard, played a pivotal role in the company. It was Gandalf who convinced Thorin to embark on the quest to reclaim Erebor, and he provided guidance and protection throughout the journey.

Óin

Óin was the older brother of Glóin, cousin of Dwalin and Balin, and uncle of Gimli. Óin's uncle was Fundin, the father of Balin and Dwalin. Óin fought in the Battle of Azanulbizar along with Thorin Oakenshield, and in TA 2941 joined Thorin's company on the quest to reclaim the Lonely Mountain, wearing a brown hood. Óin was one of the Dwarves who bet that Bilbo would come along with them to reclaim the Lonely Mountain. Óin and his brother Glóin were usually in charge of preparing the fire for the Company, as they were the most skilled of the group. He fought in and survived the Battle of Five Armies, and later settled in the Lonely Mountain with King Dáin II Ironfoot, along with many other Dwarves of the company of Thorin.
